1. The Bernoulli Structure

A Bernoulli differential equation is a classic non-linear first-order structure that looks almost linear, except for a power function of the dependent variable $y^n$ multiplying the right side.

The Bernoulli Template: $$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n$$

Boundary Constraints: The exponent $n$ can be any real number, with two exceptions:

  • If $n = 0$, the equation is immediately Linear (solve via Chapter 3).
  • If $n = 1$, the equation can be factored directly via Separable Variables (solve via Chapter 2).

Therefore, this specialized reduction technique is specifically reserved for instances where $n \neq 0$ and $n \neq 1$.

2. Mathematical Proof of Linearization

The core philosophy of this method is to apply a substitution that scales down the non-linear term, flattening the entire equation into a standard linear format.

Algebraic Derivation:
Step 1: Eliminate the non-linear power on the right side. Divide every term in the equation by $y^n$ (multiply by $y^{-n}$): $$y^{-n}\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y^{1-n} = Q(x) \quad \text{--- (Eq. 1)}$$ Step 2: Assign the substitution variable. Let our new variable $v$ replace the middle dependent grouping: $$v = y^{1-n}$$ Step 3: Differentiate $v$ with respect to $x$ using the Chain Rule: $$\frac{dv}{dx} = (1-n)y^{-n}\frac{dy}{dx} \implies \frac{1}{1-n}\frac{dv}{dx} = y^{-n}\frac{dy}{dx}$$ Step 4: Substitute back into Eq. 1. Replace the corresponding $y$ components with our $v$ terms: $$\frac{1}{1-n}\frac{dv}{dx} + P(x)v = Q(x)$$ Multiply through by the scalar $(1-n)$ to reveal a completely Linear Equation ready for an Integrating Factor: $$\frac{dv}{dx} + (1-n)P(x)v = (1-n)Q(x)$$

3. Comprehensive Lecture Example

Let's map out a complete analytical solution for a typical non-linear system.

Problem Dossier: Find the general solution for $y(1+xy^4)dx - dy = 0$.

Step 1: Rearrange into the Bernoulli template.
$$dy = (y + xy^5)dx \implies \frac{dy}{dx} - y = xy^5 \quad \text{[Here } n = 5\text{]}$$ Step 2: Divide by $y^5$ and set up the substitution.
$$y^{-5}\frac{dy}{dx} - y^{-4} = x$$ Let $v = y^{1-5} = y^{-4}$. Its derivative is $\frac{dv}{dx} = -4y^{-5}\frac{dy}{dx} \implies -\frac{1}{4}\frac{dv}{dx} = y^{-5}\frac{dy}{dx}$.

Step 3: Substitute to linearize the equation.
$$-\frac{1}{4}\frac{dv}{dx} - v = x \implies \frac{dv}{dx} + 4v = -4x$$ Step 4: Solve via the Integrating Factor method.
$$I(x) = e^{\int 4 dx} = e^{4x}$$ $$\frac{d}{dx}[v \cdot e^{4x}] = -4xe^{4x} \implies v e^{4x} = \int -4xe^{4x} dx$$ Applying integration by parts ($\int u\,dw = uw - \int w\,du$) yields:
$$v e^{4x} = -xe^{4x} + \frac{1}{4}e^{4x} + C$$ Divide through by $e^{4x}$ to isolate the intermediate solution $v$: $$v = -x + \frac{1}{4} + Ce^{-4x}$$ Step 5: Back-substitute $v = y^{-4}$ to finalize the solution.
$$y^{-4} = -x + \frac{1}{4} + Ce^{-4x}$$
