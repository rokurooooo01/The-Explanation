1. The Linear Blueprint

A first-order differential equation is linear if it can be written in a form where the dependent variable $y$ and its derivative $\frac{dy}{dx}$ are strictly to the first power, and are never multiplied together.

To solve a linear equation, it must first be mathematically normalized into the Standard Form:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)$$

The Coefficient Trap: If there is an algebraic function multiplied by $\frac{dy}{dx}$ (for example, $x\frac{dy}{dx}$), you must divide every single term in the equation by that function before identifying $P(x)$ and $Q(x)$. Failing to do this is the most common cause of calculation errors.

2. Mathematical Proof of the Integrating Factor

The goal of this method is to multiply the standard equation by an unknown master function $I(x)$ such that the left side simplifies perfectly into a single product-rule derivative.

Derivation Steps:
Multiply the standard form by an integrating factor $I(x)$: $$I(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + I(x)P(x)y = I(x)Q(x) \quad \text{--- (Eq. 1)}$$ We want the left-hand side of Eq. 1 to match the expanded calculus Product Rule for $\frac{d}{dx}[I(x)y]$: $$\frac{d}{dx}[I(x)y] = I(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + I'(x)y \quad \text{--- (Eq. 2)}$$ For Eq. 1 and Eq. 2 to be identical, look at the coefficients of $y$. We require: $$I'(x) = I(x)P(x) \implies \frac{dI}{dx} = I(x)P(x)$$ Isolate variable $I$ and integrate via separation of variables to solve for it: $$\int \frac{1}{I} dI = \int P(x)dx \implies \ln|I| = \int P(x)dx$$ Exponentiate both sides to uncover the master formula for the Integrating Factor: $$I(x) = e^{\int P(x)dx}$$

3. Operational Protocol & Verification

Once $I(x)$ is computed, the entire differential equation condenses into:

$$\frac{d}{dx}[I(x)y] = I(x)Q(x)$$

You can then integrate both sides with respect to $x$ directly to isolate and extract $y$.

Calculus Lecture Example:
Find the general solution for: $\frac{dy}{dx} - \frac{1}{x}y = x \quad (x > 0)$

Step 1: Check Form. It is already in standard form. Identify components:
$$P(x) = -\frac{1}{x}, \quad Q(x) = x$$ Step 2: Compute $I(x)$.
$$I(x) = e^{\int -\frac{1}{x} dx} = e^{-\ln(x)} = e^{\ln(x^{-1})} = x^{-1} = \frac{1}{x}$$ Step 3: Collapse and Rewrite. Multiply through and write as a product derivative:
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{1}{x} \cdot y\right] = \frac{1}{x} \cdot x \implies \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{y}{x}\right] = 1$$ Step 4: Integrate and Isolate.
$$\int \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{y}{x}\right] dx = \int 1 dx \implies \frac{y}{x} = x + C$$ $$y = x^2 + Cx$$
