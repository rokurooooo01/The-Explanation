1. The Total Differential & Exactness

An equation of the form $M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0$ is considered Exact if it is the total differential of some hidden multivariable function $f(x,y) = C$. By calculus definition, the total differential is:

$$df = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}dx + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}dy = 0$$

This implies that $M(x,y)$ is the partial derivative of $f$ with respect to $x$, and $N(x,y)$ is the partial derivative of $f$ with respect to $y$. To prove an equation is exact without knowing $f$ yet, we rely on Clairaut's Theorem of mixed partial derivatives.

The Exactness Test: $$\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}$$

2. Function Recovery Protocol

Once you verify exactness, your mission is to reconstruct the original function $f(x,y)$ from its partial derivatives. The operational loop is as follows:

  1. Integrate $M$: Integrate $M(x,y)$ with respect to $x$ (treating $y$ as a constant). Because $y$ was held constant, your integration constant is an unknown function of $y$, denoted $g(y)$.
  2. Differentiate: Take the partial derivative of your new $f(x,y)$ with respect to $y$.
  3. Equate & Isolate: Set this derivative equal to $N(x,y)$. This allows you to solve for $g'(y)$ by canceling out identical terms.
  4. Integrate $g'(y)$: Integrate $g'(y)$ with respect to $y$ to find the missing component $g(y)$.
  5. Final Assembly: Write the complete implicit solution as $f(x,y) = C$.

3. Comprehensive Lecture Example

Let's map out the function recovery process on a mixed algebraic system.

Problem Dossier: Solve $(2xy - 9x^2)dx + (x^2 + 2y + 1)dy = 0$.

Step 1: Execute the Exactness Test.
Identify $M = 2xy - 9x^2$ and $N = x^2 + 2y + 1$.
$$\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = 2x$$ $$\frac{\partial N}{\partial x} = 2x$$ Since $2x = 2x$, the system is Exact.

Step 2: Integrate $M$ with respect to $x$.
$$f(x,y) = \int (2xy - 9x^2) \partial x = x^2y - 3x^3 + g(y)$$
Step 3: Differentiate $f(x,y)$ with respect to $y$ and equate to $N$.
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = x^2 + g'(y)$$ Set this equal to our original $N(x,y)$ matrix:
$$x^2 + g'(y) = x^2 + 2y + 1$$ The $x^2$ terms perfectly cancel (this *must* happen if exact), leaving:
$$g'(y) = 2y + 1$$
Step 4: Integrate to recover $g(y)$.
$$g(y) = \int (2y + 1) dy = y^2 + y$$
Step 5: Final Assembly. Assemble the components into $f(x,y) = C$.
$$x^2y - 3x^3 + y^2 + y = C$$
