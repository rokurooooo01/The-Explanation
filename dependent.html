1. The Dependent Architecture

A standard first-order non-homogeneous equation often presents itself as a rational fraction composed of two straight-line equations:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f\left(\frac{a_1x + b_1y + c_1}{a_2x + b_2y + c_2}\right)$$

Before applying any advanced transformations, you must test the relationship between the coefficients of $x$ and $y$. The system is Linearly Dependent (Parallel) if their coefficients are directly proportional.

The Dependency Test: $$\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \quad \text{or} \quad a_1b_2 - a_2b_1 = 0$$

If this constraint is met, the lines never intersect. This structural symmetry allows us to collapse the complex $x$ and $y$ groups into a single substitute variable.

2. The $u$-Substitution Protocol

Because the coefficients are proportional, you can factor out a scalar constant to reveal identical algebraic groupings. By substituting this group out, you degrade the equation back into a standard Separable form.

  1. Identify the Core: Locate the shared algebraic group $ax + by$.
  2. Set the Sub: Let $u = ax + by$.
  3. Differentiate: Derive with respect to $x$ to find the replacement for $\frac{dy}{dx}$:
    $\frac{du}{dx} = a + b\frac{dy}{dx} \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{b}\left(\frac{du}{dx} - a\right)$.
  4. Substitute & Separate: Plug $u$ and $\frac{du}{dx}$ back into the equation. The variables $u$ and $x$ will now be mathematically separable.

3. Comprehensive Lecture Example

Let's map out the substitution mechanics step-by-step.

Problem Dossier: Solve $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x + y + 1}{x + y + 5}$.

Step 1: Test for Dependency.
$a_1=1, b_1=1$ and $a_2=1, b_2=1$. Since $\frac{1}{1} = \frac{1}{1}$, the lines are parallel. The shared core group is $(x + y)$.

Step 2: Set the Substitution.
$$u = x + y$$ $$\frac{du}{dx} = 1 + \frac{dy}{dx} \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{du}{dx} - 1$$
Step 3: Substitute and Simplify.
$$\frac{du}{dx} - 1 = \frac{u + 1}{u + 5}$$ Isolate $\frac{du}{dx}$ by moving the 1 to the right side and finding a common denominator:
$$\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{u + 1}{u + 5} + \frac{u + 5}{u + 5} = \frac{2u + 6}{u + 5}$$
Step 4: Separate Variables and Integrate.
$$\frac{u + 5}{2u + 6} du = dx \implies \int \frac{u + 5}{2(u + 3)} du = \int dx$$ Use polynomial division or algebraic splitting on the left integral: $\frac{u + 5}{u + 3} = 1 + \frac{2}{u + 3}$.
$$\frac{1}{2} \int \left(1 + \frac{2}{u + 3}\right) du = \int dx$$ $$\frac{1}{2} \left[u + 2\ln|u + 3|\right] = x + C$$
Step 5: Back-Substitute $u = x + y$.
$$\frac{1}{2} (x + y + 2\ln|x + y + 3|) = x + C$$
